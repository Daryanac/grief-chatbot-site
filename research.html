Impact of Research on the Final Website

This page documents how survey findings, prototype testing, and instructor feedback shaped the final Lantern website.

Overview

The final Lantern website was not designed in a single step. Instead, it developed through multiple rounds of research, critique, and redesign. The process began with Survey 1, which focused on audience perceptions of AI grief support and what users needed in order to trust such a platform. Survey 2 was conducted after the preliminary website and, together with check-in feedback, helped identify weaknesses in the first web implementation. Survey 3 then evaluated later website directions and directly informed the final prototype. Across these stages, the research consistently showed that users wanted the site to feel warm, trustworthy, and emotionally clear rather than cold, corporate, or overly artificial.

Survey 1: Establishing Trust, Safety, and Tone

Survey 1 played an important role in shaping the foundation of the website. Responses showed that participants were initially skeptical about the concept of an artificial intelligence voice chatbot for grief healing. Many associated the idea with discomfort, emotional distance, or a lack of authenticity. Because of that reaction, the website needed to build trust very early and explain itself carefully.

The strongest findings from Survey 1 showed that users preferred a warm and comforting tone over a purely clinical or professional one. Participants also emphasized that safety disclaimers and privacy or consent information needed to be visible from the start. In addition, respondents strongly agreed that the website should clearly state that Lantern is supportive technology, not therapy and not a replacement for a real person. Major concerns included privacy and data misuse, emotional dependence, inaccurate or insensitive responses, and lack of human empathy.

These findings shaped the early direction of the final website by making trust and emotional safety central design priorities. As a result, the website now includes visible trust and safety language, clearer explanations of what Lantern is, and a warmer, more supportive tone throughout the landing experience.

Early Draft and Implementation Notes

According to the implementation notes, the earliest website draft functioned more as a rough collection of survey findings and key information than as a polished website. At that stage, there was no formal wireframe or finalized design guide. The page included an early hero section, safety disclaimers, product explanation, and interface ideas, but it was primarily a way to organize the initial research rather than present a refined user experience.

Later feedback pushed the project toward greater focus and clarity. One important critique from check-in feedback was that the product naming and hero messaging needed revision, and that the website needed to more clearly communicate that Lantern is a voice chatbot built around the voice of a lost loved one. This feedback led to more direct framing on the homepage and a stronger emphasis on the actual function of the product.

Survey 2 and Preliminary Website Feedback

Survey 2 was conducted after the preliminary website and was especially useful in identifying what still felt weak or unclear in the web design. Participants responded positively to websites that felt simple, organized, welcoming, and less corporate. They also liked easy navigation, visible safety features, and clear layout structure. However, the preliminary version also received important criticism that directly influenced the redesign.

One of the strongest recurring themes in Survey 2 was that the website should avoid looking too obviously AI-generated. Participants pointed to AI-looking artwork, overly artificial visuals, and layouts that felt like “ChatGPT-designed” as negative features. Others noted that the color scheme felt too cold, too white, or too sterile for a grief-support context. Respondents recommended warmer colors, more human-feeling imagery, better spacing, reduced text density, improved mobile friendliness, and more explicit explanation that Lantern is an AI-driven voice chatbot rather than a general support resource.

These findings directly affected the final site. The later design uses warmer amber and purple accents, reduces the cold minimalism of the preliminary draft, and gives more space to emotional framing, safety transparency, and clearer navigation. The website also became more explicit about what Lantern is and how it differs from other mental health or support services.

Survey 3 and the Final Prototype

Survey 3 most directly shaped the final prototype. In this round, participants compared later website options, and Website #1 was preferred by 77.8% of respondents. The comments attached to this version provide some of the clearest evidence for why the final site moved in its current direction.

Respondents said they liked the top navigation, the welcoming and supportive feeling, the emotional clarity of the headline, and the overall ease of scrolling through the site. Participants also responded positively to the purple color palette and the lantern imagery, noting that the visuals felt calming, readable, and better aligned with grief and comfort. At the same time, they still suggested improvements such as making the main image look less obviously AI-generated, adding more warmth through amber and purple accents, simplifying some information, and keeping the site friendly for mobile users.

These results had a direct effect on the final website. The chosen version preserved the stronger emotional hook, maintained the lantern theme, and reinforced the purple identity, while also incorporating warmer glow elements and stronger trust-building sections. Survey 3 also supported the continued use of top navigation and the one-page scrolling structure, since users specifically described the site as easy to navigate and clear in purpose.

How Research Changed the Final Website

Overall, the research changed both the content and the presentation of the final website. First, it made emotional trust and safety non-negotiable. Instead of presenting Lantern as only an innovative AI concept, the final site frames it as a sensitive tool that must be introduced with boundaries, consent, and clear ethical explanation. Second, the research changed the visual direction. Feedback consistently moved the design away from a colder, more artificial appearance and toward a warmer, softer, and more human-centered interface. Third, it improved content clarity. Users wanted to know immediately what Lantern is, who it is for, and how it works, which led to a more direct headline, clearer feature framing, and more visible support resources.

In this way, the final website is not simply a polished version of the first draft. It is a version that reflects repeated user concerns, preferences, and recommendations across all three research stages. Survey 1 established the emotional and ethical concerns, Survey 2 refined the preliminary implementation, and Survey 3 guided the final prototype selection and visual direction.

Conclusion

The final Lantern website was shaped by an iterative research process rather than aesthetic preference alone. User feedback repeatedly showed that a grief-support platform involving AI must feel trustworthy, emotionally careful, and visually sincere. Because of this, the final design emphasizes warm tone, visible safety language, clearer explanation of the product, stronger navigation, and a more comforting visual identity. The research did not just improve the look of the website; it made the final version more appropriate for the emotional context of grief support and more responsive to the needs of college students.
