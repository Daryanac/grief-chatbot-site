A gentle way to continue the bond

Lantern is designed for students who want to stay connected to a loved one after loss. Through conversation, voice, and memory-based personalization, it offers a private space for comfort, reflection, and guidance in their voice—while keeping consent and emotional safety visible from the start.

Choose what feels right

Some users want only voice. Others would also want a visual avatar. Lantern should respect both preferences.

Lower intensity

Voice + chatbot only

For users who want the comfort of hearing and talking with their loved one without a visual likeness. This can feel gentler, more private, and less uncanny.

  • Focus stays on the voice
  • Lower emotional intensity
  • Feels simpler and more private
Optional visual avatar

Voice + gentle avatar

For users who feel more connected when they can also see a familiar face. This would be optional, user-controlled, and clearly explained before use.

  • Optional, never forced
  • More immersive presence
  • Consent and boundaries stay explicit

Trust & safety

Designed for connection, but grounded in consent, boundaries, and support when emotions become overwhelming.

Important

  • Lantern is designed for continuing bonds. It helps users stay connected through voice-based conversation after loss.
  • Lantern is AI-generated. It is shaped by recordings, memories, and user input—it is not literally bringing someone back.
  • User consent comes first. People should clearly understand what is uploaded, generated, and stored.
  • Emotional safety matters. The experience should include boundaries, reminders, and accessible human support.
  • If the experience becomes overwhelming, Lantern should encourage breaks and direct users toward real help.
