What people told us matters most

We designed this landing page around the most common survey concerns.

Lead with safety

Users want clear disclaimers and a safety path before anything else.

Protect privacy

Privacy and data misuse are top concerns, so disclosures must be explicit and early.

Avoid dependence

We set boundaries and encourage human support to reduce emotional dependence.

Privacy & consent (shown early on purpose)

Plain-language rules that match user expectations.

What we do

  • Ask for consent before collecting any personal information.
  • Let users review, edit, and delete saved “Grief Context Memory.”
  • Show clear disclosures on both the landing page and onboarding.

What we do not do

  • We do not claim to replace therapy, clinicians, or real relationships.
  • We do not enable voice replication by default.
  • We do not use private chats to train models without explicit opt-in.
Voice feature is optional. If offered, it is strictly opt-in, consent-gated, and designed to avoid deception (the system must not claim to be the loved one).

Consent & Privacy Summary

Based on survey feedback, we keep disclosures simple and visible.

  • This tool is supportive technology, not therapy and not a replacement for a person.
  • Consent is required before any personal data is stored.
  • Saved memory is user-approved and can be edited or deleted at any time.
  • Voice replication (if offered) is never the default and requires explicit opt-in consent.
