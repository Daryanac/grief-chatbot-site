A gentle way to continue the bond

Lantern is designed for students who want to stay connected to a loved one after loss. Through conversation, voice, and memory-based personalization, it offers a private space for comfort, reflection, and guidance—while keeping consent and emotional safety visible from the start.

Research Impact

How user feedback shaped the final Lantern website from early concept to final prototype.

Design iteration summary

Our final prototype was shaped directly by research

Lantern did not stay fixed from the first draft. Early research revealed skepticism toward AI grief support, strong concern around privacy and emotional dependence, and a clear need for warm tone, visible safety disclaimers, and transparency about what the product actually is. Later website testing refined the visual direction, content hierarchy, and final landing page.

77.8% preferred Website #1 in Survey 3
92.6% wanted consent/privacy shown on both landing + onboarding
88.9% were concerned about emotional dependence
Survey 1 · Early concept

What we learned first

The earliest survey showed that many students were hesitant about an AI voice chatbot for grief. Users wanted the site to feel warm and comforting instead of clinical, and they strongly emphasized trust, privacy, boundaries, and reminders that Lantern is supportive technology—not therapy and not a replacement for a real person.

  • Warm, comforting tone mattered more than clinical language
  • Safety disclaimers needed to appear early
  • Consent and privacy needed to be highly visible
  • Emotional dependence and insensitive responses were major concerns
Survey 2 · Preliminary site

How the preliminary site was adjusted

Website testing on the preliminary version pushed the design toward a cleaner and more human-feeling experience. Participants asked for less obviously AI-generated imagery, less cluttered text, warmer colors, better mobile friendliness, and clearer explanation that Lantern is an AI voice chatbot rather than a general support page.

  • Reduce the cold, corporate feeling
  • Use warmer visual accents and softer backgrounds
  • Make the landing page more transparent about the product
  • Improve readability, spacing, and mobile usability
Survey 3 · Final prototype direction

What directly shaped the final website

The final prototype was based most directly on Survey 3. Respondents preferred Website #1 because it felt more comforting, more expressive, more complete, and more clearly targeted to college students. They specifically responded well to the purple palette, the lantern metaphor, the emotional headline, and the sense of warmth and trust.

  • Keep the purple identity, but introduce warmer amber glow accents
  • Strengthen the emotional hook on the landing page
  • Use clearer student-centered visuals and language
  • Keep navigation simple and the structure easy to scroll
From data-heavy draft to guided website

The earliest version functioned more like a dump of survey insights and key information. As feedback came in, the website became more focused, clearer in purpose, and more intentional in how it introduced grief support, safety, and product expectations.

From vague framing to explicit voice-chatbot identity

Feedback from class check-ins and survey responses made it clear that the site needed to say more directly that Lantern is a voice-based AI companion shaped by a loved one’s voice, memories, and personality—not simply a general grief support resource.

From cool minimalism to warmer trust-building design

The final design introduces more amber and purple glow, stronger emotional framing, cleaner sectioning, and visible support resources. This directly responds to requests for more warmth, more humanity, and less of a sterile or AI-generated feel.

Design changes visible in the final site

  • Stronger hero headline and clearer value proposition
  • Warmer amber + purple visual accents tied to the lantern theme
  • Dedicated trust, safety, FAQ, and campus resource sections
  • Simple top navigation and one-page scroll structure
  • Option framing for voice-only versus avatar-supported experiences

Why this mattered

Because the concept is emotionally sensitive, research did not just improve appearance. It shaped the project’s trustworthiness. The final website communicates what Lantern is, who it is for, how it works, and what protections are built in—while keeping the interface more welcoming for college students.

Trust & safety

Designed for connection, but grounded in consent, boundaries, and support when emotions become overwhelming.

Important

  • Lantern is designed for continuing bonds. It helps users stay connected through voice-based conversation after loss.
  • Lantern is AI-generated. It is shaped by recordings, memories, and user input—it is not literally bringing someone back.
  • User consent comes first. People should clearly understand what is uploaded, generated, and stored.
  • Emotional safety matters. The experience includes boundaries, reminders, and accessible human support.
  • If the experience becomes overwhelming, Lantern should encourage breaks and direct users toward real help.
